Around
the
world,

Growing vegetables can be difﬁcult. It is sometimes hard to know

helps people to grow many vegetables in a small area.

Native Americans have stories about these vegetables. According to

are the middle sister, and pumpkins are the youngest sister. When
Native Americans grow these vegetables, they plant corn ﬁrst. Next,
they plant beans, and they plant pumpkins last.
If people plant the corn ﬁrst, the beans can use the corn plants
to grow and get light from the sun. The pumpkins grow along the
ground,

corn

Pumpkins also have large leaves. They are important because they
protect the area around the vegetables from the sun. Because of
this, the ground does not become too dry. These three vegetables
help each other to grow.
When Europeans went to North America in the 1600s, they
took new plants and animals with them. Since then, the food that
Native
Americans
eat
has
changed.
However,
many
Native
Americans and other farmers still use the Three Sisters Method
today.
