2. The green one.
3. The blue one.

【正解1b】
3. Bring something to eat for the volunteers.

【解説1b】

本文では、掃除のあと職員がボランティア全員に昼食（lunch）を出す、とあります。これに対応するのが選択肢3です。
飲み物はボランティアが自分で持参するため、職員が飲み物を売る・手袋を捨てる・ペットボトルを買う、という話は本文にありません。

本文3
The Three Sisters

Around the world, people grow many kinds of vegetables. Growing vegetables can be difﬁcult. It is sometimes hard to know how much sun and water they need. Native American* people in North America have a traditional method* for growing vegetables. It is called the “Three Sisters Method.” The Three Sisters Method helps people to grow many vegetables in a small area.

In the Three Sisters Method, three different vegetables are grown together. The vegetables are corn, beans, and pumpkins. Native Americans have stories about these vegetables. According to them, the vegetables are “sisters.” Corn is the oldest sister, beans are the middle sister, and pumpkins are the youngest sister. When Native Americans grow these vegetables, they plant corn ﬁrst. Next, they plant beans, and they plant pumpkins last. If people plant the corn ﬁrst, the beans can use the corn plants to grow and get light from the sun. The pumpkins grow along the ground, so animals cannot go near the corn and beans easily. Pumpkins also have large leaves. They are important because they protect the area around the vegetables from the sun. Because of this, the ground does not become too dry. These three vegetables help each other to grow.

When Europeans went to North America in the 1600s, they took new plants and animals with them. Since then, the food that Native Americans eat has changed. However, many Native Americans and other farmers still use the Three Sisters Method today.

問題3a:
When people use the “Three Sisters Method,” they can

選択肢3a:
1. learn about hundreds of Native American foods.
2. use plants to get clean water for drinking.
3. grow vegetables without light or water.
4. grow many vegetables in a small area.

【正解3a】
4. grow many vegetables in a small area.

【解説3a】
設問は、「Three Sisters Method」を使うと人々が何ができるかを聞いています。
本文の前半で、この方法は狭い場所でも多くの野菜を育てられる、と説明されています。正解は選択肢4です。
光や水がまったく不要、数百種類の食べ物を学ぶ、という内容は本文にありません。

問題3b:
What do people plant ﬁrst when they use the Three Sisters Method?

選択肢3b:
1. Pumpkins.
2. Sunﬂowers.
3. Beans.
4. Corn.

【正解3b】
4. Corn.

【解説3b】
設問は、Three Sisters Method で最初に植える野菜を聞いています。
植え順の説明で、まずトウモロコシ（corn）、次に豆（beans）、最後にかぼちゃ（pumpkins）とあります。
ひまわり（sunflowers）は本文に出てこないため、選択肢2は誤りです。

問題3c:
What do the pumpkins’ leaves do?

選択肢3c:
1. They protect the soil from the sun.
2. They help to keep the soil dry.
3. They protect the corn from the beans.
4. They help animals to ﬁnd the corn.

【正解3c】
1. They protect the soil from the sun.

【解説3c】
設問は、かぼちゃの葉（pumpkins' leaves）の役割を聞いています。
本文では、大きな葉が野菜のまわりを日差しから守り、地面が乾きすぎないようにする、と書かれています。
「土を日差しから守る」がこれに最も近い表現です。豆やトウモロコシを守る・動物を近づける、という説明とは異なります。

問題3d:
What happened after Europeans went to North America?

選択肢3d:
1. Native Americans began to eat different foods.
2. Native Americans stopped using the Three Sisters Method.
3. Europeans said pumpkins should be eaten on Halloween.
4. Europeans said Native Americans should eat more fruit.

【正解3d】
1. Native Americans began to eat different foods.

【解説3d】
設問は、ヨーロッパ人が北米に来たあとに起きたことを聞いています。
本文では、新しい動植物を持ち込んだあと「それ以来ネイティブアメリカ人が食べる食べ物は変わった」とあります。
メソッドをやめた、ハロウィンや果物の指示、という話は本文にありません。

問題3e:
What is this story about?

選択肢3e:
1. A new way to cook vegetables.
2. A special way to grow vegetables.
3. Three farmers who were sisters.
4. Three women who started a food company.

【正解3e】
2. A special way to grow vegetables.

【解説3e】
設問は、本文全体の内容（What is this story about?）を聞いています。
全文は、トウモロコシ・豆・かぼちゃを一緒に育てる伝統的な「Three Sisters Method」について述べています。
料理の新しい方法や、実在の三姉妹の農家・会社の話ではなく、「野菜を育てる特別なやり方」が要約としてふさわしいです。

本文6
La Tomatina

Buñol is a small town in Spain with a long history. Only around 10,000 people live in Buñol, and it is a peaceful town most of the year. However, for one day of the year, the number of people in the town increases by over 20,000. People from all over the world go to Buñol to celebrate a tomato festival called La Tomatina and throw tomatoes at each other.

La Tomatina began in an interesting way. In 1945, there was a parade happening in Buñol, and musicians were performing in the street. Some kids pushed a musician, and they stopped the parade. The musician got angry at the kids, and then someone threw a tomato. Many people started throwing tomatoes at each other. They had fun and tried to throw tomatoes again the next year. However, the government did not like this new event and stopped it for a while. Many people in Buñol wanted to throw tomatoes, so the festival happened again in 1957. People from other countries heard about La Tomatina and wanted to try throwing tomatoes, too.

Now, at La Tomatina, people throw a lot of tomatoes at each other for one hour. Many people wear white clothes. The color of their clothes changes because of the tomatoes. Although La Tomatina is famous, some people do not think it is good because it is hard to clean up after the festival. Also, people throw away all of the tomatoes. Some people think it is better to eat the tomatoes and not throw them at each other.

問題6a:
How many people live in Buñol?

選択肢6a:
1. About 10,000 people.
2. About 20,000 people.
3. About 22,000 people.
4. About 50,000 people.

【正解6a】
1. About 10,000 people.

【解説6a】
設問は、Buñol に住む人の数を聞いています。
「Only around 10,000 people live in Buñol」とあります。正解は約1万人です。
祭りの日に町に来る人は2万人以上増える、という別の数字と混同しないようにしましょう。

問題6b:
What happened during the parade in 1945?

選択肢6b:
1. Children played music at school.
2. Children danced in the street.
3. A musician bought tomatoes at the market.
4. A musician got angry at some children.

【正解6b】
4. A musician got angry at some children.

【解説6b】
設問は、1945年のパレード中に起きたことを聞いています。
子どもたちが音楽家にぶつかりパレードが止まり、音楽家は子どもたちに腹を立て、そのあと誰かがトマトを投げた、とあります。
子どもが演奏した・踊った・市場でトマトを買った、という話は本文にありません。

問題6c:
What do a lot of people do at La Tomatina?

選択肢6c:
1. Perform a dance.
2. Eat tomatoes.
3. Wear white clothes.
4. Go to a farm.

【正解6c】
3. Wear white clothes.

【解説6c】
設問は、La Tomatina で多くの人がすることを聞いています。
「Many people wear white clothes」とあり、トマトで服の色が変わる、とも続いています。
トマトを食べる・農場に行く・踊る、が祭りの主な描写として書かれているわけではありません。

問題6d:
Some people do not like La Tomatina because

選択肢6d:
1. there are too many people.
2. cleaning up after the festival is difﬁcult.
3. it is too expensive.
4. the tomatoes are not delicious.

【正解6d】
2. cleaning up after the festival is difﬁcult.

【解説6d】
設問は、La Tomatina を好まない人の理由を聞いています。
本文では「祭りのあとの片付け（cleaning up）が大変だからよいと思えない人もいる」とあります。
人が多すぎる・高い・トマトがまずい、という理由は本文にありません。

問題6e:
What is this story about?

選択肢6e:
1. A parade in 1945.
2. A new city in Spain.
3. A famous festival.
4. A farming event.

【正解6e】
3. A famous festival.

【解説6e】
設問は、本文全体の主題を聞いています。
Buñol のトマト祭り La Tomatina の始まり・現在・賛否が中心です。
1945年のパレードだけの話や、新しい都市・農業イベントの紹介ではなく、「有名な祭り」が最もふさわしい要約です。

Ansel Adams

Ansel Adams was a famous photographer in the United States. He was born in 1902. As a child, he could not focus in class and did not like school. Adams enjoyed taking walks in nature and playing the piano. Adams played the piano for over ten years in his childhood and was very good. He wanted to be a professional musician.

In the 1910s, Adams received his ﬁrst camera. He took many pictures in a large park in the United States called Yosemite National Park. Adams often went hiking in the mountains there with his camera. When he was older, he joined a club of people who worked in the park and went on hikes with them. Adams loved to take pictures of the mountains in Yosemite National Park.

In the late 1920s, Adams decided to become a professional photographer instead of a professional musician. Then he made many books of his photos and sold them. His photos of beautiful nature were very popular. Many people thought the photos were very artistic and were like paintings, so they became popular. Adams worked for many companies and took pictures of new products. However, Adams did not like taking pictures for companies because he believed photography was an art. He took photographs of nature and showed them in museums. Adams taught many people how to take pictures. In 1980, Adams received an award from the president of the United States to celebrate his life. Adams became one of the most famous photographers.

学校が好き・都市を歩く・絵を習う、という記述は本文と合いません。

【解説9b】

ヨセミテ国立公園で「山にハイキングに行き、カメラを持っていった」とあります。
建物の写真・カメラ店での仕事・写真クラブへの加入、という流れではありません。

問題9c:
Why were Adams’s photographs popular?

選択肢9c:
1. They were photos of old products.
2. They looked like paintings.
3. They were in many newspapers.
4. They were not expensive.

【正解9c】
2. They looked like paintings.

【解説9c】
設問は、Adams の写真が人気だった理由を聞いています。
人々は自然の写真を「絵のようで芸術的（like paintings）だと思い、人気になった」とあります。
古い商品の写真・新聞掲載・値段の安さが理由として書かれているわけではありません。

博物館での展示や著書出版・政府勤務がその年だった、とは書かれていません。
